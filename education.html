Thesis: "Trust Management System for the Internet of Things (IoT)", Grade A

The thesis addresses a fundamental vulnerability in IoT deployments: nodes that behave correctly for an initial period to accumulate positive trust, then exploit that trust to mount On-Off attacks against peer nodes. The research objective was to detect and mitigate such attacks in a multi-service IoT environment without a centralized trust authority.

  • Proposed the Dynamic Distributed Trust Management Scheme (DDTMS), in which each node is autonomous in decision-making and evaluates trust dynamically after every transaction. The scheme classifies misbehaving nodes into low, mild, and severe severity levels for proportionate isolation.
  • Designed and implemented DTMSim-IoT, a .NET-based simulation framework, to address the absence of standardized IoT trust simulation tools. The tool is open-sourced at github.com/swhamdani/DTMSim-IoT.
  • Published at IEEE DASC 2020 (8 citations) and in the Turkish Journal of Electrical Engineering and Computer Sciences (11 citations).
  • NUST Award of Publication (2022): cash award of Rs. 33,600 recognizing the ACM Computing Surveys publication arising from this work.
