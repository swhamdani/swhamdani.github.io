Biography

I am a cybersecurity researcher with interests in IoT trust management, blockchain-based network security, and network resilience. I hold an MS in Software Engineering (CGPA 3.55/4.0, Grade A thesis) from the National University of Sciences and Technology (NUST), Islamabad, where my research produced the DTMSim-IoT simulation framework, now open-sourced on GitHub.

My work has been published in ACM Computing Surveys (Q1, 96 citations) and the Turkish Journal of Electrical Engineering and Computer Sciences, and presented at the IEEE International Symposium on Dependable, Autonomic and Secure Computing (DASC 2020) in Calgary, Canada. I am currently developing the BENSAM framework, a blockchain-enhanced network scanning and monitoring system, with a journal paper published in 2025 and a conference paper in preparation.

I serve as General Secretary of the Internet Society Pakistan Islamabad Chapter, was selected as an ISOC Community Fellow in 2025, and am mentoring an Early Career Fellow through the ISOC Foundation in 2026. I am a peer reviewer for the Journal of Network and Computer Applications (Elsevier) and have taught cybersecurity at several Pakistani universities as visiting faculty since 2020.

Alongside research, I run CyberWalk.pk, a cybersecurity awareness platform, and write regularly for national newspapers and digital publications on cybersecurity and technology policy.
