About

Research Profile

I am a cybersecurity researcher and practitioner with over two decades of experience spanning applied research, academic engagement, and industry engineering. My research centers on how trust, resilience, and verifiability can be engineered into distributed and networked systems — from IoT deployments to blockchain-backed security infrastructure.

My work has been published in Q1 journals including ACM Computing Surveys (96 citations) and presented at IEEE conferences across three continents. I hold an MS in Software Engineering from NUST, where my thesis on distributed IoT trust management earned Grade A and produced both a journal publication and an open-source simulation tool.

Beyond the lab, I serve as General Secretary of the ISOC Pakistan Islamabad Chapter, was selected as an ISOC Community Fellow 2025, and mentor an Early Career Fellow through the ISOC Foundation in 2026. I am also a Visiting Faculty member at Pakistani universities, a reviewer for Elsevier's Journal of Network and Computer Applications, and a science communicator through CyberWalk.pk and national print media.

Currently Working On

  • BENSAM-Tool: extending the blockchain-enhanced network monitoring framework into a policy-aware, vulnerability-assessment system (manuscript in preparation, 2026)
  • Research into AI-driven anomaly detection integrated with distributed ledger audit trails
  • Open collaboration on IoT trust frameworks and network resilience — open to co-authorship and PhD research partnerships

Research Statement

How do we know whether a networked device, a scan result, or a security event can be trusted? This question sits at the core of my research. I study how trust can be computed, propagated, and verified across heterogeneous IoT environments and how blockchain-based architectures can provide tamper-evident guarantees for network security operations. The underlying conviction is that security is not merely a technical property to be bolted on, but a systemic quality that must be designed in from the ground up — across protocols, architectures, and the communities that depend on them.
