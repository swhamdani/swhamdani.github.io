Research Statement

What I Study and Why

How do we know whether a networked device, a scan result, or a security event can be trusted? This question sits at the core of my research. I study how trust can be computed, propagated, and verified across heterogeneous IoT environments — and how blockchain-based architectures can provide tamper-evident guarantees for network security operations. The underlying conviction is that security is not merely a technical property to be bolted on, but a systemic quality that must be designed in from the ground up, across protocols, architectures, and the communities that depend on them.

My approach integrates formal trust modelling, simulation-based validation, and prototype implementation, ensuring that findings translate from theory to testable, deployable systems. Ongoing work extends this into AI-assisted anomaly detection and policy-aware monitoring frameworks.

Dynamic Distributed Trust Management Scheme for the Internet of Things
Hamdani, S. W. A., Khan, A. W., Iltaf, N., Bangash, J. I., Bangash, Y. A., & Khan, A.
Turkish Journal of Electrical Engineering & Computer Sciences, 29(2), 796–815.
