Thesis: "Trust Management System for the Internet of Things (IoT)" — Grade A

This research addresses a fundamental vulnerability in IoT deployments: nodes that behave correctly for an initial period to accumulate positive trust, then exploit that trust to mount On-Off attacks against peer nodes. The objective was to detect and mitigate such attacks in a multi-service IoT environment without reliance on a centralized trust authority.

  • Proposed DDTMS: a Dynamic Distributed Trust Management Scheme in which each node is autonomous in decision-making and communicates directly with peers. Trust is evaluated dynamically after every transaction, enabling rapid detection of behavioral changes.
  • Attack detection: the scheme successfully detects On-Off attacks and classifies misbehaving nodes into three severity levels (low, mild, severe) for proportionate isolation responses.
  • Simulation gap addressed: no standardized standalone IoT simulation tool existed for validating trust management schemes. Designed and implemented DTMSim-IoT — a .NET-based simulation framework — as the test bench, now open-sourced on GitHub.
  • Performance: simulation results show improved time-to-detection and reduced interaction overhead compared to state-of-the-art schemes.
  • Published: IEEE DASC 2020 (8 citations) and Turkish Journal of Electrical Engineering & Computer Sciences (11 citations).
