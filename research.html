Research Interests

My research addresses the question of how trust, resilience, and verifiability can be engineered into distributed and networked systems. I study trust computation and propagation in heterogeneous IoT environments, blockchain-based architectures for tamper-evident network security operations, and AI-assisted anomaly detection integrated with distributed ledger audit trails. The underlying position is that security must be designed in from the ground up rather than added after the fact.

Research areas: IoT Security and Trust Management  ·  Blockchain-Based Network Security  ·  Network Resilience and Monitoring  ·  Distributed Systems Security  ·  AI/ML for Cybersecurity  ·  Cybersecurity Standards and Compliance  ·  Privacy Protection  ·  Federated Learning

Journal Articles

Blockchain-Enhanced Network Scanning and Monitoring (BENSAM) Framework
Hamdani, S. W. A., Ali, K., and Muhammad, Z.
Blockchains, 4(1), 1. 2025.
Presents a framework integrating blockchain technology with network scanning operations to provide tamper-proof, auditable records of network security events, device discovery, and vulnerability assessments. Smart contract logic enables automated anomaly flagging without reliance on a centralized trust authority.
DOI: 10.3390/blockchains4010001
Cybersecurity Standards in the Context of Operating System: Practical Aspects, Analysis, and Comparisons
Hamdani, S. W. A., Abbas, H., Janjua, A. R., Shahid, W. B., Amjad, M. F., Malik, J., Murtaza, M. H., Atiquzzaman, M., and Khan, A. W.
ACM Computing Surveys, 54(3), Article 57, 1-36. 2021.[Q1, IF approx. 14, 96 citations]
A comprehensive survey and comparative analysis of cybersecurity standards as applied to operating systems, including NIST, CIS, FIPS, and ISO 27001. Presents a detailed literature review, analysis of cyber-attack statistics, and a practical compliance mapping framework developed from the WOSCoT toolkit.
DOI: 10.1145/3442480
Dynamic Distributed Trust Management Scheme for the Internet of Things
Hamdani, S. W. A., Khan, A. W., Iltaf, N., Bangash, J. I., Bangash, Y. A., and Khan, A.
Turkish Journal of Electrical Engineering and Computer Sciences, 29(2), 796-815. 2021.[11 citations]
Proposes a Dynamic Distributed Trust Management Scheme (DDTMS) for IoT networks that detects and mitigates On-Off attacks. The scheme enables autonomous node-level trust computation evaluated dynamically after every transaction, classifying misbehaving nodes into severity levels. Validated using the DTMSim-IoT simulator.
DOI: 10.3906/elk-2003-5

Conference Papers

DTMSim-IoT: A Distributed Trust Management Simulator for IoT Networks
Hamdani, S. W. A., Khan, A. W., Iltaf, N., and Iqbal, W.
18th IEEE International Symposium on Dependable, Autonomic and Secure Computing (DASC 2020), pp. 491-498. Calgary, Canada. 2020.[8 citations]
Presents DTMSim-IoT, a .NET-based standalone simulation framework for testing and validating distributed trust management schemes in IoT networks. Fills a gap in the absence of standardized IoT trust simulation tools, enabling researchers to implement and benchmark static and dynamic trust models. Source code is open-sourced on GitHub.
